History of blockchain

The blockchain method was initially developed in 1991, but not intended for the purpose it is used for today (uses will be discussed further in the report). In 1991, research scientists Stuart Haber and W. Scott Stornetta in their peak of scientific developments, were attempting to develop a platform on which digital documents could be stored and backed up to allow easy access. The blockchain back then was used to make the timestamps of the documents and to make them ultimately more secure so they can’t be manipulated or changed. However, the blockchain model was further developed in 2008 by a team under the control of a man named Satoshi Nakamoto. It has not been confirmed however, it is alleged that his team developed the pre-existing blockchain network to use it as a digital ledger system for transaction. And this gave rise to the much-heard cryptocurrency Bitcoin. And in 2009, the ‘newly developed’ blockchain method was utilised and a transaction was created under the chain to man named Hal Finey, and since the, just under the Bitcoin crypto network over 750 million trades have occurred under the blockchain network just for one cryptocurrency (bitcoin). This is just one example which indicates that the blockchain network was a great success in launching such a well renowned cryptocurrency and start a foundation for other digital currencies to be born.

Benefits

In the current world, the reliance on technology and electronic transaction is increasing exponentially every year. As the demand for online transaction increases the burden on central clearing authority is increasing resulting in long wait times for large transfers (for example to purchase a house) the transfer may take 2-3 days depending on the amount and requiring clearance by authorities. While on the other hand, using the decentralized ledger system (blockchain), the transaction occur across a peer-to-peer network, this allows parties to have transactions cleared in a matter of minutes without requiring approvals and clearance authentication from authorities. Blockchain records are immutable meaning that they are subject to zero manipulation and alteration as it will just not allow the future transactions to be tracked. This key feature of blockchain allows it to be used in a very important way, keeping track of medications and prescribed drugs to individuals who require regular prescriptions. When talking about prescriptions, the record keeping for the collected medication and the stock take could vary due to human error in record keeping. So much so, that according to a research article published by The University of Nottingham, focused on investigating the errors which occur in pharmacies on prescribing. They found that over a 12-month period, ‘around one in eight patients were exposed to a prescribing or monitoring error’. This shows that in such a small size of 1777 patients, mismanagement of prescriptions is a big problem and a waste to society. Keeping record of medication is very important to reduce misuse and prevent wastage in addition to minimising losses for the public health network.

Disadvantages

At present, although many large organisations have implemented or are in the process of implementing the blockchain network into their business activates there are some limitations that some may face.

- Poor adoption: Due to the young technology which only began widespread use in 2009 and primary intended for cryptocurrency it hasn’t quite entered the widespread community yet. However, as more business see that their business will be enhanced through the use of this technology it will be adopted more.
- Difficulty to make revisions: One disadvantage at present is that there is no possible way to make revisions to the Blockchain. If a clerical error does occur, it is impossible to revise the transaction and instead will require a whole new transaction to refund/reimburse the error. This may deter businesses from this technology as it will increase the clerical time required.
- Looks complicated: The blockchain is not very easy to express and understand in laymans terms and hence it is hard to get the older generation on board which also contributes to the reduced uptake of this techonology.
